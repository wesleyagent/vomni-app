Booksy has strong brand recognition in the UK barbering market. Walk into any high street barbershop and there's a decent chance they're on it. But for independent operators — solo barbers or small shops — the economics deserve a closer look before you commit.

## The Core Problem with Booksy for Independents

Booksy's base plan costs around £29.99/month. That's reasonable. The problem is the add-on that most barbers feel pressured to use: Booksy Boost.

Boost promotes your listing within the Booksy marketplace to attract new clients. The fee: up to 30% commission per booking. On a £25 cut, that's £7.50. Do 20 of those in a month and you've paid £150 in commissions on top of your monthly subscription.

The business model is designed around scale: Booksy makes more as you grow, which is the opposite of what a successful indie barber needs.

## What Independent Barbers Actually Need

The requirements for a two-chair barbershop are different from a multi-location salon chain:

- Simple, mobile-friendly booking that clients can access from a WhatsApp link or Instagram bio
- Automated reminders so you don't have no-shows eating your day
- Review automation to build your Google profile without manually asking every client
- Predictable, flat-fee pricing — not a percentage of your revenue

## Booksy Alternatives for UK Barbers

**Vomni** — Built specifically for independent barbershops. Flat monthly fee, no commission, no marketplace. Booking links that work in WhatsApp and Instagram. Automated review requests sent post-appointment. The focus is on helping you own your client relationships — not rent them from a directory.

**Shedul / Fresha** — Free plan available, but carries the 20% new-client commission if you use the marketplace. Good UI, widely used. The pricing model is the main concern.

**Treatwell** — Positioned at the salon market. Strong marketplace traffic in London. Commission-based model (around 30% for new clients). Works if you're heavily reliant on walk-in discovery, less good if you're building a repeat-client base.

**Setmore** — Genuinely free for basic use. No commission, no booking fees. The free version has feature limits. The paid tier is around £9/month. A solid option for a solo barber just getting started.

## The Marketplace Dependency Problem

Both Booksy and Treatwell present the same fundamental risk: your business becomes dependent on their platform for client acquisition. When their algorithm changes, when they raise commission rates, or when a competitor outbids you for placement — your bookings drop and you have no recourse.

The barbers building the most durable businesses in 2026 are the ones who've invested in:

1. A strong Google Business Profile with consistent 5-star reviews
2. A direct booking link they own and control
3. An email or WhatsApp list of clients they can contact without paying a platform

## Switching from Booksy: What to Do First

1. **Export your client list** — Booksy allows CSV export. Do this before you cancel.
2. **Update your Google Business Profile** with your new booking link.
3. **Update your Instagram and WhatsApp Business bio** to point to the new link.
4. **Send your existing clients a message** letting them know where to book — most will follow without issue.

The migration takes a few hours. The savings compound from month one.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Booksy free for barbers?**
The base plan starts at around £29.99/month. Booksy Boost adds commission on top — up to 30% per promoted booking.

**Can I use Booksy without Boost?**
Yes. The base subscription gives you a booking page and scheduling tools without Boost. You won't appear in Booksy's marketplace discovery without it, though.

**Will I lose clients by switching booking platforms?**
Rarely. Clients book with you, not with your software. If you send a WhatsApp message with your new link, the vast majority of regulars will rebook there without hesitation.

**What's the fastest way to build bookings without a marketplace?**
Google Business Profile plus consistent Google reviews plus a direct booking link in your bio. Within 3–6 months this typically generates more organic bookings than marketplace placement at zero ongoing cost.
