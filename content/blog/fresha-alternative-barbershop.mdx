Fresha's free plan sounds too good to be true — because it is. The moment a new client books through the Fresha marketplace, you're charged a 20% commission on that booking. For a £30 haircut, that's £6 gone before you've touched scissors. Here's what the alternatives actually look like.

## Why Barbers Leave Fresha

The free-plan hook is effective. Most barbers sign up, set up their profile, and don't notice the commission structure until they see their first payout statement. The issues barbers consistently report:

- The 20% new-client commission applies to every client who finds you through Fresha's marketplace — even clients who later rebook directly
- No data ownership — your client list belongs to Fresha, not you
- Price increase history — Fresha introduced commissions in 2022 after years of being genuinely free; there's no reason to believe rates stay fixed
- Upsell pressure — Fresha Premium unlocks features (like blast messaging your own clients) that most booking platforms include by default

None of this makes Fresha a bad product. It's well-designed and widely used. But the economics work for Fresha, not for the business owner.

## What to Look for in a Fresha Alternative

Before comparing platforms, get clear on what actually matters:

1. **Flat monthly fee vs. commission** — A flat fee is predictable. Commission is a tax on growth.
2. **Client data ownership** — Can you export your client list and contact them directly?
3. **Automated follow-up** — Rebooking reminders and review requests shouldn't require manual effort.
4. **No marketplace dependency** — You should be able to build bookings through your own links, not a third-party directory.

## Fresha Alternatives Worth Considering

**Vomni** — Flat monthly fee. No commission on any booking, new or returning. Native booking system with automated review requests, rebooking reminders, and lapsed-client reactivation. Designed specifically for barbershops and salons in the UK and Israel. You own your client data.

**Timely** — Strong scheduling platform popular with salons. Monthly subscription, no per-booking fees. Better suited to multi-staff operations. Less automation around reviews and retention.

**Acuity Scheduling** — Flexible booking tool used across industries. Not barber/salon-specific. Good for solo operators who want simplicity over features.

**Square Appointments** — Free for individuals. Takes a small payment processing fee but no booking commission. Limited automation. Best for barbers who already use Square for payments.

## The Marketplace Trade-Off

The argument for staying on Fresha is marketplace visibility — new clients can find you through their directory. This is real, but worth examining:

- Fresha marketplace clients are less loyal (they're price-shopping across providers)
- Every client you acquire through Fresha at 20% cost teaches them to book via Fresha next time — not via your own link
- A Google Business Profile and a direct booking link generates leads at zero per-booking cost

For most independent barbershops, owning their own booking channel and investing in Google visibility outperforms marketplace dependency within 12–18 months.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does Fresha charge for existing clients?**
No. The 20% commission only applies to new clients who discover you through the Fresha marketplace. Direct bookings from existing clients are not charged.

**Can I export my client list from Fresha?**
Yes, Fresha allows client data exports. Do this before switching to any alternative.

**Is there a booking platform that's genuinely free with no hidden fees?**
Square Appointments is free for individual operators, with standard payment processing fees. It's limited in automation features but has no booking commissions.

**What happens to my Fresha reviews if I switch?**
Fresha reviews stay on Fresha's platform. Your Google reviews travel with your Google Business Profile regardless of which booking software you use — another reason to prioritise Google over platform-specific reviews.
