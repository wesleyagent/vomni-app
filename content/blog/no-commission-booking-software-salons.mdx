Commission-free booking software charges a fixed monthly fee — typically £30–£80 — rather than taking 20–30% of every new-client booking. For a salon with 25 new clients per month at a £40 average ticket, the difference is **£200–£300/month in retained revenue**.

## Why Salons Are Moving Away from Commission Models

The marketplace booking model — where platforms like Fresha and Booksy offer free or cheap software in exchange for a percentage of new-client bookings — made sense when salons had no online presence and needed marketplace discovery.

In 2026, most salons generate their new clients through:
- Google Maps (clients searching "salon near me")
- Instagram and TikTok
- Word of mouth
- Their own website

For these salons, paying 20% commission to a marketplace for clients who would have found them anyway is simply a tax on growth.

## The Break-Even Calculation

The decision is mathematical:

**Monthly commission cost** = New clients/month × Average ticket × Commission rate

**Flat-fee software cost** = Monthly subscription

When your commission cost exceeds your subscription cost, you're better off on a flat-fee platform.

The crossover for most salons is around **8–12 new clients per month from marketplace sources**.

## What "No Commission" Actually Means for Your Business

Switching to a commission-free platform doesn't just save money on each booking — it changes your relationship with your clients:

**Client ownership:** On commission platforms, your clients are also the platform's clients. They'll market competing salons to your clients. On a flat-fee platform, your client list is yours alone.

**Pricing control:** Commission platforms sometimes display promotional pricing or comparison features that affect how clients perceive your rates. With direct booking, you control the entire experience.

**Data portability:** Flat-fee platforms generally offer better data export tools, making future migrations easier.

## Making the Switch

Migrating from a commission platform to a flat-fee alternative typically involves:

1. Exporting your client list from the current platform
2. Importing into the new platform (most accept CSV)
3. Updating your booking links (Google Business Profile, Instagram bio, website)
4. Notifying existing clients with your new booking link

Most clients follow you without friction — they booked with *you*, not with the platform.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is commission-free salon booking software?**
Commission-free salon booking software charges a flat monthly subscription rather than taking a percentage of each booking. You pay one predictable fee regardless of how many bookings you take or how many new clients you attract.

**What are the best no-commission alternatives to Fresha?**
The main no-commission alternatives to Fresha are Vomni (flat monthly fee, built for barbers and salons), Square Appointments (free for solo, £29/month for teams), Timely, and Acuity Scheduling. Each suits different business sizes and needs.

**Is no-commission booking software worth it for small salons?**
It depends on your new-client volume. If you're acquiring fewer than 10 new clients per month through a marketplace, commission-based platforms may be cheaper. Once you exceed 12–15 new clients per month, flat-fee software almost always costs less.

**Can commission-free booking software integrate with Google Maps?**
Yes. Platforms like Vomni and Square Appointments support Google Reserve, allowing clients to book directly from your Google Business Profile — without going through a marketplace that charges commission.

**What happens to my client data if I switch booking software?**
Your client data belongs to you. Most platforms allow CSV export of your client list, which you can then import into your new platform. The migration process typically takes 1–2 hours.
